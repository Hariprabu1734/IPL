Indian Premier League

The Indian Premier League (IPL) is a men's Twenty20 (T20) cricket league in India that's held annually. It was established in 2008 by the Board of Control for Cricket in India (BCCI) to promote domestic cricket internationally. The league is based on a round-robin group and knockout format, and features teams from major Indian cities.

Some other details about the IPL:

The IPL has become one of the most popular and lucrative cricket leagues in the world, attracting top players from around the globe. During matches, fans can enjoy Bollywood music and entertainment from cheerleaders during intermissions. The league is organized by eight cricket teams in March and May of each year.
